SmoothOperator

Your semi-autonomous luggage assistant for enhanced accessibility in hospitality environments.

Staffing Shortage & Mobility Impaired Inaccessibility

Post-COVID staffing shortages in the hospitality sector have increased mobility challenges, especially for the 61 million Americans living with disabilities. SmoothOperator is an interactive robotic assistant engineered to autonomously and safely transport luggage in environments such as airports and hotels. Our end-to-end system integrates custom hardware and software, including SLAM-based navigation, dynamic obstacle avoidance, and layered safety redundancies. Powered by a Jetson Nano & a dual-battery system, and controlled via onboard touchscreen or a secure mobile app, it supports payloads up to 80 lbs and speeds up to 1.2 m/s.

System Overview & Installation

Block Diagram

User Interfaces

Onboard Touchscreen: A 21.5" display showing a friendly face with eyes/mouth animations, menu navigation, QR scanning, and lift controls.

Physical Setup

Chassis: 24"×24"×57" 80/20 aluminum with foam bumpers, dual DC barrel jacks, and a compound gear train.

Setup & Support

Operation

Power On/Off

Connect DC barrel jacks, verify LEDs on Jetson & controllers, engage e-stop remote. To shut down, use on-screen shutdown then unplug batteries.

Tele-Operation

  1. Open mobile app; enter 4-digit passcode shown onboard.
  2. Use on-screen gamepad for speed, lift, and drive controls.

Autonomous Mode

  1. Press screen → Main Menu → Scan Boarding Pass.
  2. Align QR code; confirm success (green flash).
  3. Accept navigation; robot plans path & avoids obstacles.
  4. At destination, option to return home.
  5. In emergencies, remote e-stop halts all motion.

Technical Deep Dive

Electronics & Power

Dual 12V 12Ah batteries in parallel feed a fused distribution board. Subsystems are protected by dedicated fuses.

Motor & Locomotion

Two DC motors with 10:1 gear reduction, controlled via RoboClaw 15A. Encoders & MPU6050 IMU drive odometry.

Sensor Suite

360° RPLiDAR A1 for SLAM, HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensors, and limit-switch bumpers. Logitech C270 camera for QR scanning.

Communication & Control

ROS navigation stack with SLAM, AMCL, Dijkstra's global planner & DWA local planner. HTTP & WebSocket bridge to frontends.

Visual & Safety Indicators

RGB LED ring indicates motion state. Hardware e-stop and bumpers provide redundant safety stops.

Installation & Setup

Hardware: Plug in dual batteries; verify voltage; engage e-stop. For charging, use the intelligent charger.

Software: ROS Melodic on Ubuntu, Python 3.13.3 + Kivy, Node.js 16 & Expo Go for mobile.

Engineering Standards

  • IEEE 802.11/15: Wireless networking protocols
  • NEC & NESC: Electrical safety and grounding
  • ISO 12100: Machinery risk assessment and protective measures
  • ADA: Accessibility guidelines for public spaces
  • ISO/IEC Software & HTTP/JSON/WebSocket for reliable interfacing

Cost Breakdown

Beta build cost: $1,918.75. Key components provided by sponsors included the LiDAR, Jetson Nano, and 8020 extrusions.
