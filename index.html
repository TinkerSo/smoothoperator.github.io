SmoothOperator

Your semi-autonomous luggage assistant for enhanced accessibility in hospitality environments.

Executive Summary

Post-COVID staffing shortages in the hospitality sector have increased mobility challenges, especially for the 61 million Americans living with disabilities. SmoothOperator is an interactive robotic assistant engineered to autonomously and safely transport luggage in environments such as airports and hotels. Our end-to-end system integrates custom hardware and software, including SLAM-based navigation, dynamic obstacle avoidance, and layered safety redundancies. Powered by a Jetson Nano & a dual‑battery system, and controlled via onboard touchscreen or a secure mobile app, it supports payloads up to 80 lbs and speeds up to 1.2 m/s.

System Overview & Installation

Block Diagram

Our architecture comprises five processors—Jetson Nano (navigation & UI), Arduino Mega (motor & safety), Arduino Uno (ultrasonic), plus auxiliary modules—networked via serial, USB, and Wi‑Fi.

Physical Setup

Chassis: 24"×24"×57" 80/20 aluminum, pool‑noodle bumpers with limit switches, dual DC barrel power jacks, and a compound gear train (10:1 reduction).

Images needed: As-built robot (assets/images/robot_full.jpg), lifting mechanism (assets/images/lift_mechanism.jpg), drivetrain (assets/images/drivetrain.jpg), bumper (assets/images/bumper.jpg), electronics suite (assets/images/electronics.jpg).

Setup & Support

Hardware: Plug in dual 12 V batteries; ensure each shows ~12.6 V. Activate e‑stop for actuators. For charging, use the intelligent charger (assets/images/battery_charger.jpg).

Software: Install ROS Melodic on Ubuntu (Jetson Nano), Python 2 for ROS nodes, Python 3.13.3 + Kivy for UI, Node.js 16 & Expo Go for mobile app.

Operation

Power On/Off

Connect DC barrel jacks, verify indicator LEDs on Jetson & controllers, engage e‑stop remote. To shut down, use on‑screen shutdown, then unplug batteries.

Image needed: DC jack connection: assets/images/dc_jack.png

Tele-Operation

  1. Open mobile app; enter 4‑digit passcode shown onboard.
  2. Use on‑screen gamepad for speed, lift, and drive controls.

Image needed: App lock & control screens: assets/images/app_lock.png, assets/images/app_control.png

Autonomous Mode

  1. Press screen → Main Menu → Scan Boarding Pass.
  2. Align QR code; confirm success (green flash).
  3. Accept navigation; robot plans path & avoids obstacles.
  4. At destination, option to return home.
  5. In emergencies, remote e‑stop halts all motion.

Images needed: Boarding pass scan & confirm: assets/images/scan_pass.png, assets/images/confirm_autonomous.png

Technical Background

Electronics & Power

Dual 12 V 12 Ah batteries in parallel feed a fused distribution board. Subsystems are protected by dedicated fuses (logic 5 A, motors 10 A).

Images needed: Power distribution diagram: assets/images/power_distribution.png

Motor & Locomotion

Two DC motors with 10:1 gear reduction, controlled by RoboClaw 15 A via Arduino Mega. Encoders (8192 CPR) + MPU6050 IMU drive odometry.

Image needed: Gear train and encoder assembly: assets/images/gear_train.jpg

Sensor Suite

360° RPLiDAR A1 for SLAM, HC-SR04 ultrasonics for low‑height obstacles, limit switches under foam bumpers, Logitech C270 for QR scanning.

Images needed: Sensor cluster: assets/images/sensor_suite.jpg

Communication & Control

ROS nodes handle SLAM, AMCL localization, global/local planning (Dijkstra & DWA), recovery behaviors, and serial commands to microcontrollers. Frontend communicates via HTTP & WebSocket.

Image needed: ROS & backend feedback loops: assets/images/ros_architecture.png, assets/images/communication_diagram.png

Visual & Safety Indicators

RGB LED ring under chassis (green = motion, red = halted). Hardware e‑stop and bumper interrupts act as redundant safety layers.

Image needed: LED indicator demo: assets/images/led_ring.jpg

Engineering Standards

  • IEEE 802.11/15: Wi‑Fi & Bluetooth networking
  • NEC & NESC: Power distribution & grounding
  • ISO 12100: Machinery risk assessment & protective measures
  • ADA: Accessible design in public spaces
  • ISO/IEC Software & HTTP/JSON/WebSocket protocols

Cost Breakdown

Beta build cost: $1,918.75. Key contributors were 2D LiDAR, Jetson Nano, chassis hardware, and electronics provided by sponsors.
