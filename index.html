Problem & Vision

In the wake of COVID-19, airports and the hospitality sector have seen a significant drop in staffing — making services like luggage assistance more difficult to access. At the same time, a growing demographic is being left behind.

These compounding issues have left millions struggling to navigate airports independently. From long wait times for assistance to physical barriers in terminals, the lack of scalable solutions leaves many with mobility challenges behind.

Our Vision

We envision a future where mobility challenges are no longer barriers to travel. SmoothOperator is our response — a semi-autonomous robotic assistant designed to help travelers transport their luggage safely, independently, and with dignity.

Equipped with intelligent navigation, obstacle detection, and an easy-to-use interface, our robot transforms the travel experience for individuals with limited mobility, elderly passengers, and anyone overwhelmed by today’s under-resourced airport environments.

SmoothOperator supports independent navigation and lifting for all.

System Design

Architecture Overview

Subsystems Overview

  • Mechanical: Lift platform, chassis, 10:1 gear train.
  • Electrical: Dual 12 V batteries, motor drivers, safety fuses.
  • Software: ROS navigation stack, touchscreen UI, mobile app.

Implementation & Results

Mechanical Construction

The chassis is built from 80/20 T-slot aluminum extrusions, providing a lightweight yet robust frame. A 10:1 compound gear train and high-torque DC motors deliver the torque needed to transport up to 80 lbs, while pool-noodle bumpers with limit switches add passive collision protection.

Sensor Suite

We integrate a 360° RPLiDAR A1 for SLAM, HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensors under the chassis for low-lying obstacle detection, wheel encoders (8192 CPR) and an MPU6050 IMU for odometry, plus a Logitech C270 camera for QR boarding-pass scanning.

Navigation System

The ROS navigation stack combines AMCL-based localization, Dijkstra’s global planner and DWA local planner, and custom recovery behaviors. Velocity commands are sent over serial to an Arduino Mega, ensuring real-time path following and obstacle avoidance.

User Interface

Onboard, a 21.5" touch screen runs a Kivy-based UI with friendly facial animations and boarding-pass scanning. Remotely, a React Native mobile app (paired via a 4-digit code) provides tele-operation gamepad controls and lift commands.

Electronics Architecture

Dual 12 V 12 Ah lead-acid batteries in parallel feed a fused distribution board. A Jetson Nano handles high-level processing, while two Arduinos manage sensors and motor controllers, all networked via USB/UART and Wi-Fi.

Safety Protocols

Layered safety features include hardware bumpers with kill-switch limit triggers, ultrasonic blind-spot detection, a radio-frequency e-stop, and software watchdogs in the ROS stack to halt operations on critical faults.

Version 0: Initial Proof-of-Concept

Our first prototype focused on basic locomotion, remote control, and structural integrity. Built using a simple chassis and manual control system, V0 served as a functional feasibility test of motor capabilities, wheelbase stability, and weight-bearing performance.

  • Custom-cut 80/20 aluminum frame
  • Basic dual-motor drivetrain
  • Manual lift operation
  • Arduino-controlled with joystick input

Final Prototype: Fully Integrated Smart Assistant

The final SmoothOperator system integrated hardware and software subsystems, enabling semi-autonomous operation. The robot can scan boarding passes, plan routes, lift luggage, and navigate terminals while ensuring user safety.

  • SLAM-based localization and navigation
  • Touchscreen-based interface and passcode pairing
  • Linear actuator with safe overload lift design
  • ROS-powered control and custom Android app
